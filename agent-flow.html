Knowledge Transfer Agent

How the Agent Works

Five steps from "enter your email" to a published wiki page. Each step maps to something one of us builds on Wednesday.

5 Steps ~15 Minutes 1 Published Doc
1

Someone enters their email

Glean Integration

The user types in their work email. Behind the scenes, we look up everything they've written — docs, wiki pages, their role and team — using enterprise search (Glean).

Think of it like: A doctor pulling your chart before the appointment. Before we ask a single question, we already know what you've been working on.
📧 email → profile, docs, expertise summary
Context gathered — preparing AI
2

The AI gets prepped

Captain + Interview Designer

All that context plus a structured interview question tree gets fed to Claude. Now the AI knows who you are, what you've worked on, and what to ask.

Think of it like: A journalist who did their homework before the interview. They've read everything you've published — so they skip the obvious and go straight for what isn't written down.
🧠 context + questions → tailored AI interviewer
AI ready — starting conversation
3

A guided conversation

Chat UI Builder + Captain

10–15 turn chat. The AI asks smart questions, references their actual docs, follows up when something interesting comes up. A progress bar tracks coverage.

AI Interviewer
I see you authored 6 docs about the billing pipeline. Which ones are still accurate?
You
The runbook from 2024 is completely wrong now. The deploy process changed when we moved to K8s.
AI Interviewer
Walk me through the current deploy process — the one that isn't written down anywhere.
You
Sure. First you need to check the feature flags in LaunchDarkly, then...
6 / 8 topics covered
Interview complete — synthesizing
4

A handoff doc is generated

Doc Template + Synthesis

A separate AI call takes the full transcript and produces a structured handoff document — responsibilities, risks, undocumented processes, contacts, open questions.

Not a transcript. A structured, organized knowledge map — the kind of doc you'd actually want to read if you were taking over someone's role.
📝 transcript + template → structured handoff doc
Document ready — publishing
5

Published to Confluence

Confluence Publisher

Preview the doc, click "Publish," Confluence page appears. Done. 15 minutes of their time, and their team has a knowledge map forever.

🚀  Publish to Confluence
✓ Published → confluence.company.com/wiki/knowledge-transfer/jane-chen-handoff

💡 The Bigger Play

People don't have to be leaving to use this. Quarterly brain dumps build a living knowledge profile over time. When someone does leave, the handoff is already 80% done before anyone gives notice. That's the vision we pitch to judges — not just an exit tool, but an ongoing knowledge capture system for the entire org.
